New problem statement:

Can predator-prey population dynamics emerge in a neuroevolutionary agent-based simulation without hard-coded ecological strategy, and under what parameter conditions are these dynamics most reliably produced?


We’ve looked into the literature into what exactly predator prey dynamics are and we’ve found these consistent behaviors: 
Predator and prey populations oscillate 
Prey population peaks consistently precede predator peaks
Red Queen theory: both species increase in fitness across generations

Statistical Framework & Success Criteria

Test 1: Do populations oscillate?

Predator-prey systems (Lotka-Volterra) predict cyclic rise and fall in population counts.
This test analyzes preySurvivors per generation.
Remove long-term trend from preySurvivors 
Compute autocorrelation across generation shifts (lags)
Look for a negative dip at a non-trivial lag
Pass criterion: Cyclic signal at lag ≥ 3 generations with autocorrelation r < −0.15

Test 2: Does prey lead predator?

Changes in prey population should precede predator response by a consistent delay. Detrended preySurvivors and predatorSurvivors
Compute cross-correlation between prey and predator series over multiple lags
Identify the lag where the association is strongest
Run permutation test with 2,000 circular shifts to build a null distribution
Pass criterion: prey-leads-predator offset with permutation p < 0.05.








Test 3: Are both species improving over time?

Red Queen coevolution predicts both species keep adapting in a persistent arms race. 
The test uses meanFitnessPrey and meanFitnessPredator per generation. Linear regression of fitness vs. generation index for each species.

Pass criterion: positive fitness trend for both predator and prey (p < 0.10).


Below we performed an experiment with our baseline configurations over 45 generations, during the simulation we observed some local predator, prey behavior like predators forming groups and prey fleeing from attacks. We also performed our statistical analysis, and saw that with the baseline configurations there was an oscillation in population for prey survival. However it failed two other statistical tests for prey population leading the prey, and there was no improvement over time of the fitness functions.
